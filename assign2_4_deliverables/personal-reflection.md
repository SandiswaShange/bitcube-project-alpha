# Sprint 1 — Personal Reflection

## Key Learning
The biggest lesson I learned is that work during a sprint does not always move as quickly as expected, even when stories seem simple during planning. Unexpected challenges, testing, and dependencies can slow progress, which means tracking progress regularly is important.

## Assumptions That Failed
The biggest assumption that failed was estimation. The team underestimated how much work would be required to handle booking conflicts, testing, and integrating filtering functionality, which affected the amount of work completed in the sprint.

## Behavioural Change
In Sprint 2, I would spend more time breaking stories into smaller tasks and checking progress earlier to avoid falling behind near the end of the sprint.

## Professional Growth
I need to strengthen my estimation and sprint planning skills so I can better judge how much work is realistic to complete in a sprint and identify risks earlier.
